Relative and Absolute

  • Original position is always relative to its direct closest parent
  • Its children will always be relative to them because they have defined position.
  • Its children is relative to the adjusted position of the parent, not original position of the parent

Relative

  • Simply adding relative will not change anything
  • Adjusting top/left/bottom/right will move its display relative to its original place, but its orginal place is still occupied
  • Simple definition: Relative to its original position

Absolute

  • Simply adding wont move it from its original position, but its parents and siblings will ignore its existence.
  • Its children will still be relative to it
  • The size of absolute elements equals its own content, ignoring its children's size
  • Adjusting top/left/bottom/right goes relative to the closest parent that has a position defined
  • Children are relative to the moved absolute parent, not the original position of absolute parent
  • Simple definition: Taking out from html, but yet relative to parents with position defined.
